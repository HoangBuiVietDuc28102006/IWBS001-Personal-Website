Hanoi

Hanoi is a city that is proudly and entirely Vietnamese, despite centuries of French and Chinese influence. From the Old Quarter, which has French colonial structures, to the more than 600 temples and pagodas scattered around the city, its complex past is evident wherever you look. At places like the Ho Chi Minh Mauseoleum and Hoa Lo Prison, you may go straight to the city's history. Experience it all on foot (or by scooter), allowing time for stops at some of the more recent additions to the city, such as independent art galleries, boutique stores, and hip cafés. You're not the only one that is here for the food. While banh mi and bun cha are undoubtedly essential, make time for additional street food favourites like shrimp cakes, stir-fried eel, crispy crepes, and a few cold beers to wash it all down.

I love this place because Hanoi was the place where I lived and I had a lot of memories there with my family and friends.

Visitors to Nghe An may explore historical treasures, ecological zones, zoos with a variety of rare species, picturesque beaches, and ecological tourism places where they can connect with nature.

Sapa

In the middle of a demanding mountain hike or rice-paddy trip, the vibrant and quaint market town of Sapa in the north-west offers the ideal respite. As a reminder of the town's French missionary heritage, the Gothic stone church in the middle of the town stands out among the stores and booths. In addition to enjoying Vietnamese or European-inspired dining downtown, make sure to attend the Saturday night "love market," which is one of the most powerful—and rigid—single-night events in the world.

I love Sapa for its stunning terraced rice fields, cool mountain air, and the rich traditions of its welcoming hill tribe communities.

Phong Nha-Ke Bang

The 85,754-hectare Phong Nha-Ke Bang National Park was added to the World Heritage List in 2003. With this expansion, the site's total surface area has increased by 46% to 123,326 hectares, and it now borders the Hin Namno Nature Reserve in the PDR Laos. Tropical woods and limestone plateaux make up the Park's terrain. Numerous caverns and subterranean rivers are among the amazing phenomena it provides, along with a significant deal of geological variety. Numerous indigenous species and a high degree of biodiversity may be found at the location. In addition to giving the catchment areas—which are crucial for maintaining the integrity of limestone landscapes—greater protection, the expansion guarantees a more cohesive ecology.

Ha Long Bay

The Ha Long Bay-Cat Ba Archipelago, which includes 1,133 islands and islets and spans 65,650 hectares, is situated in Quang Ninh Province and Hai Phong City in northeastern Vietnam. The Ha Long Bay-Cat Ba Archipelago is a breathtaking seascape carved by nature, made up of many limestone islands and islets rising from the sea in a range of sizes and forms and offering gorgeous, pristine environment. One of the most significant regions of fengcong (clusters of conical peaks) and fenglin (isolated tower features) karst in the world is Ha Long Bay in the Cat Ba Archipelago, which is also the most well-known and widespread example of marine-invaded tower karst. Furthermore, the normal ecosystems also dominate the incredibly gorgeous environment.

Yen Tu Mountain

Northeastern Vietnam's Quang Ninh province contains the city of Uong Bi, where Yen Tu Mountain is located. Only a short drive between Hanoi (about 110 km) and Halong Bay (about 50 km) separates the two locations. With its towers, pagodas, woods, mountains, and Zen ambience, Yen Tu Mountain is a serene and new place.
